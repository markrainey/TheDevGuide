**Engineering Patterns** is the technical heart of The Dev Guide, moving beyond general tool usage into the specific architectures required to build reliable, production-grade AI systems. In 2026, the industry has shifted from "YOLO prompting" to Evaluation-Driven Development (EDD) and Context Engineering. This section explores how to move past basic chat interfaces and construct "State-Aware" systems. You will learn the transition from static RAG (Retrieval-Augmented Generation) to Agentic RAG, where models don't just fetch data but actively investigate, reason across multi-step lookups, and use "Just-in-Time" context to minimize hallucinations and manage the token budget of long-context models like Gemini 1.5 Pro.

Someone should read this section to master the transition from a "Prompt Engineer" to an AI Systems Architect. As autonomous agents move from experimental pilots to the "Enterprise OS," the real engineering challenge lies in Orchestration and Reliability. Here, we break down the design patterns for multi-agent ecosystems—such as the "Plan-Execute-Verify" loop—and the implementation of the Model Context Protocol (MCP) to provide agents with secure, real-time access to local data and external APIs. By the end of these modules, you will know how to build deterministic guardrails around probabilistic models, ensuring your AI applications are not just impressive demos, but scalable, auditable, and cost-optimized software solutions.
