The "Build" phase has shifted from "typing code" to "Reviewing and Orchestrating Agents." In 2026, 70% of new code is typically scaffolded or fully written by AI. This subsection focuses on the symbiotic relationship between the developer and their "Agentic Squads." It moves beyond simple autocomplete to explore multi-file refactoring, automated documentation as you type, and "Self-Correcting" compilers that use AI to fix syntax and logic errors in real-time.

You should read this to master the Review-Driven Development workflow. As velocity increases, the developer’s primary role becomes one of a "Senior Editor," verifying that the AI’s output matches the project's security standards and style guides. We cover how to manage "Context Drift" in large projects and how to use AI to safely migrate legacy systems into modern, AI-friendly frameworks without breaking production.

### Recommended Resources

#### 📚 Books & Guides
* [**The AI-First Developer: From Coding to Orchestrating**](https://www.oreilly.com/library/view/ai-native-development/9781098170000/) by *Example Author (O'Reilly, 2025)*.
    > **Why read:** This guide explores the "Vibe Coding" movement and provides a framework for breaking down large features into "agent-consumable" chunks.
* [**Co-Intelligence: Living and Working with AI**](https://www.penguinrandomhouse.com/books/747124/co-intelligence-by-ethan-mollick/) by *Ethan Mollick*.
    > **Why read:** Essential for understanding the "Jagged Frontier" of AI capabilities—knowing exactly where to trust an agent's implementation and where to step in.

#### 🎥 Videos & Implementation Deep Dives
* [**Building a Full-Stack App in 10 Minutes with Replit Agent**](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=RkIK5t0Jbn4) by *Replit*.
    > **Watch:** See the 2026 "Idea-to-Deploy" workflow in action, where the agent handles DB schema, backend routes, and frontend state.
* [**My LLM Coding Workflow for 2026**](https://addyosmani.com/blog/ai-coding-workflow/) by *Addy Osmani*.
    > **Watch/Read:** A masterclass in "Intent Engineering"—using a `spec.md` as the source of truth to drive agentic coding sessions without losing control.
* [**Agentic Coding: Multi-file Refactors with RooCode and Cline**](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=YAvYCyzJoug).
    > **Watch:** A technical tutorial on using open-source agents to perform complex, cross-dependency refactors that traditional "autocomplete" tools can't handle.

#### 📄 Essential Articles & Documentation
* [**The 2026 Guide to AI Agents: Tools and Frameworks Compared**](https://www.datacamp.com/blog/best-ai-agents) by *DataCamp*.
    > **Read:** A comparison of **SmolAgents**, **CrewAI**, and **OpenAI Agents SDK** for building your own custom implementation squads.
* [**Effective Context Engineering for AI Agents**](https://www.anthropic.com/engineering/effective-context-engineering-for-ai-agents) by *Anthropic*.
    > **Read:** Learn how to structure your codebase and prompts so that agents "see" the right context, reducing "hallucinated" bugs.
* [**Qodo: The Missing Quality Layer in your AI Stack**](https://www.qodo.ai/blog/best-ai-coding-assistant-tools/).
    > **Read:** Documentation on the **"PR-Agent"** pattern—how to use AI to pre-review and "sanity-check" agent-generated code before human review.
