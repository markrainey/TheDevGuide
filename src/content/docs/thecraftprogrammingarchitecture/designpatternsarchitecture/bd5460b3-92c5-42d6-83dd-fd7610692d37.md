This course offers a comprehensive journey into designing microservices architectures, focusing on evolving systems from monolithic to advanced event-driven and serverless models. The primary goal is to equip learners with the ability to design highly available, scalable, low-latency, and resilient systems capable of handling millions of requests. The learning methodology is highly interactive and practical, following a "Problem -> Learn -> Design -> Code -> Evaluate" cycle that encourages active participation in architectural decision-making and understanding the 'why' behind each choice.

The curriculum systematically progresses through various architectural styles, starting with basic monolithic designs and evolving through 3-Tier, Modular Monolithic, and different stages of microservices, including event-driven and serverless implementations. Key topics covered span microservices decomposition, diverse communication patterns (sync/async, API Gateways), data management strategies (polyglot persistence, CQRS, Event Sourcing), transaction management (SAGA), distributed caching, and robust deployment strategies using containers, orchestrators, and CI/CD. The course also delves into over 30 essential microservices design patterns and principles, such as Database-per-Service, Backends for Frontends, Service Mesh, and resilience patterns like Circuit Breaker, alongside observability and monitoring techniques.

With a strong emphasis on hands-on design activities, participants will collaboratively refactor architectures step-by-step, applying best practices to meet stringent functional and non-functional requirements. This blend of theoretical knowledge and practical application, including code reviews and technology choice discussions, aims to provide real-world experience. Geared towards beginners familiar with software architecture basics, the course prepares individuals for software architecture and system design interview exams by fostering a deep, practical understanding of modern distributed system design.
