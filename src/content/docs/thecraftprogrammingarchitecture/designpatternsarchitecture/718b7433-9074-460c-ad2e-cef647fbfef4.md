Modern system design faces significant challenges centered around data, including issues of scalability, consistency, reliability, efficiency, and maintainability. The complexity is compounded by an overwhelming variety of available tools, such as relational databases, NoSQL datastores, and message brokers, making it difficult for engineers to select appropriate solutions and understand the underlying principles.

Author Martin Kleppmann addresses this complexity in his comprehensive guide. The book helps navigate the diverse landscape of data processing and storage technologies by examining their pros and cons. By focusing on fundamental principles rather than just fleeting buzzwords, it equips software engineers and architects to apply core ideas in practice and effectively utilize data in modern applications.

Readers will learn to operate existing systems more effectively, make informed decisions by identifying the strengths and weaknesses of different tools, and understand the crucial trade-offs related to consistency, scalability, fault tolerance, and complexity. The guide also delves into the distributed systems research underpinning modern databases and offers insights from the architectures of major online services.
