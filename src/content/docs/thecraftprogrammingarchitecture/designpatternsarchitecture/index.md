Architecture is the art of drawing lines between components to manage complexity. Without a solid structural foundation, even the most brilliant algorithms can become trapped in a "Big Ball of Mud." This subsection covers the timeless Gang of Four patterns—like Strategy, Factory, and Observer—as well as modern architectural paradigms such as Microservices, Event-Driven Design, and Hexagonal Architecture.

The goal here is to provide a vocabulary for solving recurring problems. By implementing proven design patterns, teams can communicate more effectively and build systems that are decoupled and resilient to change. We focus on the "why" behind the "how," helping you decide when to reach for a heavy-weight microservice architecture and when a well-structured monolith is the more pragmatic choice.

Focus: Structural integrity, decoupling, and high-level system design.

📚 Books
"Designing Data-Intensive Applications" by Martin Kleppmann: Often called "the most important book for modern developers," it covers the architecture of distributed systems.

"A Philosophy of Software Design" by John Ousterhout: A short, punchy alternative to "Clean Code" that focuses on reducing complexity.

"Architecture for Flow" (2025/2026 Edition): A modern favorite that combines Domain-Driven Design (DDD) with Team Topologies.

"Head First Design Patterns" (2nd Ed): Still the best way to learn the classic Gang of Four patterns without falling asleep.
